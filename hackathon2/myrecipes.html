Classic Spaghetti Bolognese

Rich tomato and beef sauce tossed with spaghetti.

 
    
Ingredients:

200g spaghetti

250g ground beef

1 cup tomato sauce

1 small onion, chopped

2 garlic cloves, minced

1 tbsp olive oil

Salt & pepper, to taste

Fresh basil, for garnish 



Instructions:

Cook spaghetti according to package.

Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil.

Add beef and cook until brown.

Stir in tomato sauce and simmer 15 min.

Serve over spaghetti, garnish with basil.

Injera with Doro Wat (Ethiopia)

Spongy flatbread served with spicy chicken stew.

Ingredients:


Injera:

2 cups teff flour

2 1/2 cups water

Pinch of salt

Doro Wat:

500g chicken drumsticks

2 onions, chopped

2 tbsp berbere spice

2 tbsp butter

2 hard-boiled eggs 



Instructions:


Mix teff flour and water, ferment 1–2 days, cook thin pancakes for injera.

Sauté onions, add berbere and chicken, cook with water 45 min.

Jollof Rice (West Africa)

Spicy, tomato-based rice, a West African favorite.

Ingredients:


2 cups long-grain rice

1/4 cup vegetable oil

1 onion, chopped

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 red bell pepper, blended

2 cups tomato puree

1 tsp thyme

1 tsp curry powder

2 cups chicken broth

Salt & pepper, to taste

Protein (chicken, beef, or fish) optional 



Instructions:


Heat oil, sauté onions and garlic.

Add blended pepper and tomato puree, cook 10 min.

Add spices and broth, then rice.

Cover, simmer until rice is cooked.

Serve with fried or grilled protein.

Kenyan Ugali

Staple maize meal dish, dense and slightly firm, served with stews or vegetables.

Ingredients:


2 cups maize (corn) flour

3 cups water

Pinch of salt (optional) 



Instructions:


Bring the water to a boil in a pot.

Gradually add maize flour while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.

Reduce heat to low and keep stirring until mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot.

Cook for 5–10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Shape into a round mound using a wooden spoon or spatula.

Serve hot with sukuma wiki (collard greens), nyama choma (grilled meat), or stew.
